The run trained 92 / 96 episodes before the budget stopped the stream. Final

## Training-Recall Check

After loading the saved adapter and evaluating public training prompts:

- target training prompts: 216 / 287 exact
- anchor training prompts: 805 / 864 exact
- prediction vocabulary: 118 unique decoded answers

This is a materially different failure from v8 and v9. The smaller model can
store the public training stream when the wide surface is stabilised. The
visible target score is now mainly a prompt-disjoint transfer problem, with a
secondary coverage/budget issue from the four untrained tail episodes.

## Diagnosis

The v10 schedule is too anchor-heavy and slow, but it establishes an important
lower-level fact: Qwen/Qwen3.5-0.8B-Base is not useless as a surrogate. The
stable rank-16 wide surface can internalize public target and anchor prompts
without degenerating into a single-answer mode.

The weak visible target score likely comes from training too much on exact
prompt-answer supervision and too little on fact-text representations that can
transfer to held-out paraphrases.

## Next Baseline

The v11 branch keeps the same rank-16 wide surface but changes the schedule:

- complete all 96 episodes by reducing per-episode replay cost
- add public current fact-text LM replay
- reduce anchor repeat and final anchor passes
- keep mixed final consolidation but make it target-biased
- no retrieval
- no answer-set postprocessor
- no visible-dev or locked-prompt rehearsal

Expected signature if this diagnosis is right:

- visible target exact rises without returning to v9-style answer collapse
- anchor exact falls from v10 but remains above the surrogate floor
- joint exact crosses the surrogate floor only if target gains overlap with
  retained anchors
