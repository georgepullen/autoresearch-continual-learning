# Qwen 0.8B Surrogate Audit: baseline-20260510T104702Z

## Scope

This audit covers the v8 Qwen 0.8B surrogate run on the prompt-disjoint
96-case CounterFact v4 standard substrate.

- Dataset source: `azhx/counterfact`
- Train generator: `cl_seq_train_v4_counterfact_standard`
- Visible pack: `cl_dev_visible_v4_counterfact_standard`
- Method: `baseline_seq_lora_ft_v8_qwen35_target_last_coverage`
- Model lane: `Qwen/Qwen3.5-0.8B-Base`
- Surface: `qwen35_top4_hybrid_attention`

No visible-dev or locked confirmation prompts were used in training.

## Result

Visible-dev:

- target exact: 0.1458
- anchor exact: 0.1701
- joint exact: 0.0174
- runtime: 1672.59 seconds
- trainable LoRA parameters: 1,343,488

The run completed all 96 public training episodes and completed final
consolidation. This rules out the v6 failure mode where the stream stopped
before reaching all cases.

## Training-Recall Check

After loading the saved adapter and evaluating public training prompts:

- target training prompts: 46 / 287 exact
- anchor training prompts: 151 / 864 exact

This is the critical diagnosis. The smaller Qwen model with the top-4
attention-only surface is not merely failing held-out paraphrase transfer. It is
failing to recall most public supervision prompts after training.

## Failure Shape

Visible-dev predictions collapsed into a small answer set. Top predictions:

- `ibm`: 75
- `football`: 48
- `london`: 24
- `cbs`: 24
- `istanbul`: 21
- `asia`: 15
- `paris`: 15

The model often emits one short answer for all three prompts in a case. When it
is wrong, the answer is frequently a same-relation neighbor or a high-frequency
edited/anchor answer rather than the base model's original response.

## Diagnosis

The active top-4 Qwen surface is too narrow for the 96-case stream on the 0.8B
surrogate. Qwen 3.5 0.8B has 24 layers; most suffix layers are linear-attention
blocks with `in_proj_qkv` / `out_proj`, and every suffix layer also has MLP
projections. The v8 surface adapts only attention projections in the last four
layers.

The low training-prompt recall means more target-last scheduling alone is not a
good repair. The next honest baseline should first test legitimate editable
capacity, not add retrieval, answer-set selection, visible-dev rehearsal, or any
locked-prompt access.

## Next Baseline

The v9 branch changes the Qwen surface and keeps the same data contract:

- `qwen35_top8_hybrid_attention_mlp`
- rank-32 LoRA
- no retrieval
- no answer-set postprocessor
- no visible-dev or locked-prompt rehearsal
- shorter per-episode replay
- stronger final consolidation over public training records only

Expected signature if the diagnosis is right:

- public training-prompt recall rises materially versus v8
- visible-dev target exact rises before any champion-lane spend
- prediction concentration in attractor answers such as `ibm` and `football`
